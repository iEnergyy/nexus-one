# Problem Statement

## Context

Most psychologists in the Dominican Republic — especially in Santo Domingo — run private practices without dedicated software. Therapy centers face amplified versions of the same problems across multiple therapists and shared rooms.

## How Psychologists Work Today

| Tool | Used for |
|------|----------|
| WhatsApp | Scheduling, reminders, patient communication |
| Google Calendar | Appointment tracking |
| Excel / notebooks | Payment tracking, patient lists |
| Paper / Word docs | Session notes |

## Pain Points — Solo Psychologist

### Missed appointments and no-shows
- Reminders sent manually or inconsistently
- Cancellations leave empty slots with no recovery process
- Revenue lost with no visibility into the scale of the problem

### Fragmented patient records
- Contact info in WhatsApp, history in notes app, payments in Excel
- No single view of a patient's treatment journey
- Searching past sessions is slow or impossible

### Poor financial visibility
- Unclear who owes money and for which sessions
- Monthly revenue requires manual calculation
- No dashboard to understand practice health

### Administrative overhead
- Time spent on scheduling and reminders instead of clinical work
- Duplicate data entry across tools
- No audit trail for changes or access

## Pain Points — Therapy Centers (Future)

- Coordinating multiple therapists and shared rooms
- No centralized reporting across practitioners
- Administrative staff lack role-based access to scheduling without seeing clinical notes

## Why Now

- WhatsApp is already the communication channel — patients expect it
- Psychologists are willing to pay for tools that save time and recover revenue
- No dominant practice management platform exists for Spanish-speaking LATAM therapists
- Teletherapy and marketplace models are proven in other regions but underserved locally

## What Success Looks Like

A psychologist opens NexusOne in the morning, sees today's sessions, conducts appointments, writes notes, tracks payments, and sends reminders — all without leaving the platform. Cancellations are logged, and (in Phase 2) open slots are recovered automatically.
