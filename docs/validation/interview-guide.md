# Interview Guide — Psychologists

**Duration:** 30–45 minutes  
**Format:** In-person, video call, or phone  
**Interviewer tips:** Listen more than pitch. Map answers to our three workflows.

## Before the Interview

- Review [Personas](../product/personas.md) — you're talking to "Dr. Ana"
- Have [MVP Scope](../product/mvp-scope.md) summary ready (1 page, not a demo)
- Record notes in the hypothesis log after each session

---

## 1. Warm-Up (5 min)

- Tell me about your practice — how long, how many patients, where are you based?
- Do you work alone or with other therapists?
- What tools do you use daily to manage your practice?

## 2. Current Workflow — Daily Practice Loop (10 min)

*Maps to [Daily Practice Loop](../workflows/daily-practice-loop.md)*

- Walk me through a typical day — how do you know who you're seeing?
- After a session, how and when do you write notes?
- How do you track who has paid and who hasn't?
- What's the most frustrating part of this daily routine?
- How much time per week do you spend on admin (not clinical work)?

## 3. Current Workflow — Patient Lifecycle (5 min)

*Maps to [Patient Lifecycle](../workflows/patient-lifecycle.md)*

- When a new patient contacts you, what do you do first?
- Where do you store their information?
- How do you find a patient's history from 6 months ago?
- What happens when a patient stops coming — do you keep their records?

## 4. Current Workflow — Cancellations & Reminders (10 min)

*Maps to [Cancellation & Waitlist](../workflows/cancellation-waitlist.md)*

- How often do patients cancel or no-show per month?
- Do you send reminders? How? How consistently?
- When a patient cancels, what do you do with the empty slot?
- Do you keep a waitlist? How formal is it?
- How much revenue do you estimate you lose to no-shows monthly?

## 5. Pain Ranking (5 min)

Ask them to rank these 1–5 (1 = biggest pain):

| Pain | Rank (1–5) |
|------|------------|
| Scheduling and calendar management | |
| No-shows and cancellations | |
| Session note organization | |
| Payment tracking and revenue visibility | |
| Patient communication (reminders) | |

## 6. Concept Reaction (5 min)

Briefly describe NexusOne (don't demo):

> "A single platform where you manage patients, calendar, session notes, payments, and automatic WhatsApp reminders — built for psychologists in Santo Domingo."

- What sounds most valuable?
- What's missing that you'd need on day one?
- Would you use this daily or only for some tasks?
- Have you tried other tools? Why did they fail?

## 7. Pricing (5 min)

- What do you currently spend on tools for your practice (if anything)?
- Would RD$700/month feel reasonable for this? RD$1,200/month?
- What would make it a clear "yes" vs "too expensive"?
- Would you prefer monthly or annual billing?

## 8. Close (2 min)

- Would you be interested in a pilot when we launch? (Get commitment level: yes / maybe / no)
- Can I follow up with a one-page summary?
- Do you know 2–3 colleagues who might talk to us?

---

## Center Admin Add-On Questions (5 interviews, signal only)

- How many therapists and rooms do you manage?
- What's hardest about coordinating schedules?
- Do therapists share a calendar tool or each manage their own?
- Would a center-wide dashboard be valuable? What would it show?
- What would you pay per month for center management? (RD$4,000–10,000 range)

Do not scope MVP based on center answers — log as Phase 3 signal only.

---

## After the Interview

Update [Hypothesis Log](hypothesis-log.md) with:

- Date, participant type (solo / center), patient count
- Top pain ranked #1
- Pricing reaction (yes / hesitant / no)
- Pilot interest (committed / maybe / no)
- Notable quotes
- Any MVP scope change suggestions
